I'm a researcher and statistician based in New York City, with a focus on societal systems — how we got here, and where we might go next. My work often sits at the intersection of data, equity, and design, exploring the structures that shape our lives and how we might imagine better ones. I'm always curious about people, and drawn to ideas that create more inclusive, thoughtful spaces. Here are some projects I've worked on, if anything resonates, please reach out!

Projects

  • At the Machine Learning for Good Lab, I analyze racial and socioeconomic disparities in opioid overdose outcomes to inform more equitable public health policy.
  • I'm working with Ava to create a matchmaking platform (featured in SFGate).
  • I've studied how venture capital influences the structure and accessibility of education (available as a preprint).
  • I've worked at the Brennan Center, examining how campaign fundraising pressures shape political leadership (available here).
  • Earlier in my career, I was a software engineer and a teacher with Girls Who Code — experiences that continue to inform how I build, communicate, and advocate for more accessible technology.
